Interaction Techniques for Foldable Phones

Evaluating Three Touch Gestures for Moving Objects Across Foldable Screens

Abstract

The new ‘big’ thing in smartphone innovation is the foldable screen, with many of the big names in smartphone production hopping on to the bandwagon of an OLED flexible display inspired technology. Although foldable phones are now commercially available, not much research has gone into whether the interaction techniques for these phones can be directly transferred from flat, single screen phones to foldable ones – given the fact that users may choose to interact with the foldable phones when it is folded or held at various angles. This paper adopts three techniques from previous works on touch interaction for moving objects and tests their usability in various fold angles for both portrait and landscape modes.

Study Outcome

Apart from being technologically innovative, foldable phones offer new ways for people to collaborate in a collocated manner. By creating territoriality within the extended display, users can ensure they can share specific, desired content with their collaborators without having to worry about sacrificing their privacy. Although this premise holds great promise, it is vital to understand how users view and interact with the foldable screen – particularly when it comes to moving objects across the extended display. By exploring the best interaction techniques for users to interact with foldable displays, this paper lays the groundwork for how users can use foldable phones to collaborate in a collocated manner, as well as utilise the extended display in the best manner possible. This project is published in IMWUT'22!
